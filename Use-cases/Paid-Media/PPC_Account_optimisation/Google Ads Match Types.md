Google Ads’ keyword match types have evolved significantly over the last few years, impacting how advertisers ensure _relevant and qualified traffic_. Here’s a breakdown of the **evolution** and **modern strategy**:

---

### **1. Evolution of Google Ads Match Types**

#### **Legacy Match Types (Pre-2018)**

- **Broad Match**: Very loose; matched searches with similar terms.
    
- **Broad Match Modifier (BMM)**: Used `+` signs to enforce word presence in any order.
    
- **Phrase Match**: Required keywords in a specific order within search queries.
    
- **Exact Match**: Only triggered if the exact keyword was searched (no variation).
    

#### **Major Changes (2018–2021)**

- **BMM was deprecated (2021)**: Phrase Match absorbed BMM functionality.
    
- **Exact Match became less strict**: Included close variants (e.g. plurals, misspellings).
    
- **Phrase Match updated**: Now matches queries with the same meaning or intent, not just the phrase.
    

#### **Today’s Match Types**

- **Broad Match**: Uses AI to match to _relevant intent_, not just words. High reach, lower control.
    
- **Phrase Match**: Matches queries with the _same meaning_ as your keyword in a semi-structured order.
    
- **Exact Match**: Now allows close variants but still offers the most control.
    

---

### **2. Modern Strategy for Relevance & Qualified Traffic**

With automation increasing, **manual controls have shifted to inputs, data signals, and structure**. Here's what top performance marketers are doing:

#### **A. Use of Match Types**

- **Exact Match**: For high-converting terms with intent; control cost and quality.
    
- **Phrase Match**: For discovery of mid-funnel queries with known structure.
    
- **Broad Match**: Only when paired with strong _audience signals_ (e.g. Smart Bidding + First-Party Data).
    

#### **B. Key Tactics**

1. **Leverage Smart Bidding**:
    
    - Use Target CPA or Target ROAS to let Google optimize based on intent.
        
    - Works best when paired with sufficient conversion data.
        
2. **Tight Theme Ad Groups (“SKAG Lite”)**:
    
    - Avoid pure SKAGs, but use semantically tight ad groups for ad relevance.
        
    - Combine this with tailored ad copy and high-quality landing pages.
        
3. **Use of Negative Keywords**:
    
    - Aggressively filter irrelevant traffic, especially when using Phrase/Broad match.
        
4. **First-Party & Audience Signals**:
    
    - Upload customer lists (e.g. CRM, converters, site visitors).
        
    - Use GA4 audience segments to inform bidding.
        
5. **Query-Level Performance Analysis**:
    
    - Still vital: regularly review Search Terms Report.
        
    - Pause keywords triggering low-quality queries or low CVR.
        
6. **Conversion Value Rules & Offline Conversions**:
    
    - Adjust bidding based on lead quality (especially for B2C/UHNWI leads).
        
    - Import offline conversions to close the loop.
        
7. **Campaign Segmentation by Intent & Funnel Stage**:
    
    - Separate campaigns by stage (e.g., “High Intent” vs. “Exploration”).
        
    - Use different match types and bidding strategies accordingly.

### **Example: UHNWI Audience Use Case**

If you're targeting UHNWI travel bookings:

- **Exact match for high-value, branded queries** like “luxury African safari private jet.”
    
- **Phrase match for middle funnel** like “exclusive safari holidays” + remarketing lists.
    
- **Broad match only** with audience overlays: e.g. high net worth users from GA4 segments, luxury travelers from in-market audiences.
