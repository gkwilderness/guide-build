## **What Volume Does Google’s Algorithm Need to Work Effectively?**

**Officially:**

- **15–30 conversions per campaign per month** = **minimum** for _Target CPA / Target ROAS_
    
- **50–100+ conversions/week account-wide** = _sweet spot_ for algorithmic stability
    
- Anything under that? Performance is **unstable**, especially if funnel stages or geos are mixed.
    

**For luxury safaris (~$10k+ bookings):**

- Conversion rates are low.
    
- **Form fill ≠ Booking**, so you're likely working with **assisted conversions**, not direct sales.
    
- The algorithm needs **proxy goals** (e.g., CRM score tiers, engaged sessions, itinerary submissions) to model conversion intent more effectively.
    

**Your actual use-case?**  
With 50k clicks/month:

- If even 1–2% convert = ~500–1,000 leads/month.
    
- That’s **plenty** to run Smart Bidding across MOFU/BOFU campaigns, as long as the structure is clean and the conversion actions are high quality.
    
- TOFU campaigns may remain best handled via **Max Clicks with CPC caps + audience targeting**, not automated bidding.

## **Bottom Line: What I’d Adjust**

|Area|Recommendation|
|---|---|
|Match Types|Keep exact/phrase-heavy at MOFU/BOFU; restrict Broad to small, controlled TOFU discovery tests.|
|Campaign Count|Minimize campaign fragmentation. Aim for _15–30+ conversions per campaign_ as a rule.|
|Smart Bidding|Use tCPA/tROAS only where you meet minimum conversion thresholds. Else, start with Max Clicks / Manual CPC with eCPC.|
|Conversion Strategy|Define a primary (form fill) + 2–3 secondary signals (e.g., itinerary downloads, qualified page views) to feed the algo.|
|Funnel Staging|Still use it — but group closely related terms/camps together to maintain data density.|
|Budget Distribution|Overweight BOFU/MOFU for efficiency. Keep TOFU on tight leash and heavily audience-guided.|
